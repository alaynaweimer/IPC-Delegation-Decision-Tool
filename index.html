‚è±Ô∏è The True Cost of Your Time

Your time has a dollar value. Every hour you spend on a $15/hour task is an hour you can't spend on $100/hour work.

The question isn't "Can I do this task?" ‚Äî it's "Should I be the one doing it?"

üìä The math: If you earn $100/hr and spend 5 hours on bookkeeping, that "free" work actually cost you $500 in opportunity cost. A $200 bookkeeper would save you $300.

üíº Pro Tips From the Field

  • Start with recurring tasks. A 2-hour weekly task = 104 hours/year. That's 2.5 weeks of work! Small recurring tasks add up fast.
  • Include learning curve. First time delegating a task takes longer (documentation, training). Factor that into the ROI calculation.
  • Your rate should be aspirational. Use the rate you WANT to earn, not your current rate. This pushes you toward higher-value work.
  • Quality matters. If an expert does it 20% better, that has value too ‚Äî even if they cost more per hour.
  • Mental energy counts. Tasks you dread drain more than their hours suggest. Delegate energy-draining work first.
  • Document before delegating. Create a simple SOP. It makes handoff smoother and lets you reclaim the task if needed.
